Where is the problem worst?

Zone Battery Unavailability Hotspots

Percentage of scooters unavailable per zone — the red bar marks the worst zone
Zone Z7 has the highest unavailability in the fleet at around 17.9% — nearly 1 in 5 scooters there is offline at any time. This is not random. Z7 consistently ranks worst regardless of weather or day of week, which means it is a structural resource problem, not a temporary one. Zones Z1 and Z3 also show elevated rates and need monitoring.
Redeploy technicians to Z7
2
What is causing the downtime?

Charging Response Time vs Average Downtime Per Scooter

Each point is a group of scooters binned by how long it took to respond to their low-battery alert
The data shows a near 1-to-1 relationship between response delay and downtime — correlation r = 0.849. Every extra minute it takes a technician to collect a low-battery scooter adds roughly one minute of downtime. The line rises steeply and consistently from left to right. This is the single most actionable finding: the operations team can directly cut downtime simply by responding faster to alerts.
Set 60-min response target

When does it happen — and what is the tipping point?

Downtime by Zone and Cause

Average downtime per scooter split by cause — low battery, maintenance, and unavailability
Across all zones, low-battery causes drive the most downtime — more than maintenance issues. Zone Z7 leads again. This confirms the problem is primarily a charging and collection workflow problem, not a hardware failure problem.
Battery mgmt is the priority

Battery Level vs Availability Pattern

As battery drops below 20%, downtime spikes and availability collapses to zero
The 20% battery level is the critical threshold. Below it, downtime jumps sharply and scooter availability falls to near zero. Scooters need to be collected before they reach 20% — which means alerts should trigger at 25% to give technicians a response window.
Alert at 25%, not 20%

What is all this costing the business?

Peak Hour Revenue Impact — Downtime Quartiles

Average daily revenue per scooter by downtime quartile, comparing peak hours vs off-peak
Scooters with high downtime earn $8.18 less per day during peak hours than scooters with low downtime. Both lines — peak and off-peak — fall from left to right as downtime increases. The peak-hours line drops more steeply, meaning downtime during busy periods hurts revenue the most. Across 300 scooters, this adds up to over $2,400 in lost revenue every single peak day. The fix is not complicated: get scooters charged and available before peak hours begin, not during them.
